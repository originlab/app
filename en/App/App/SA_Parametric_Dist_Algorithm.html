2.5.1.2 Algorithm: Parametric Distribution Analysis (Right Censoring)

SA-Parametric-Dist-Algorithm


Data are input in time and censoring indicator pairs .

The MLE (maximum likelihood estimation) and LS (least squares) methods are used to estimate parameters for each distribution.

Under the MLE method:

  1. First calculate the likelihood function for the distribution, which is the product of Probability Density Function for failure time data and the Survival Density Function for censored time data.
  2. Then maximize the logarithm of the likelihood function by setting its partial derivatives equal to zero with respect to parameters.
  3. Use Newton-Raphson method to solve these parameters in step 2.

Under the LS method:

  1. First plot the failure times (or log failure times) against the transformed cumulative probabilities on a probability plot.
  2. Then fit a straight line by minimizing the sum of squared deviations
  3. Use the slope and scale from the fitted line to calculate parameter estimates

Pearson Correlation Coefficient

The Pearson correlation coefficient is calculated under the Least Squares estimation method. It measures how well the straight line fits the input data. A high value indicates the least squares regression lines explains most of the variation in Y through X.
